# **Synopsis**

“Landman" immerses us in the world of oil rig workers and the powerful corporations that dictate their lives. Set against the unforgiving backdrop of West Texas, the series follows Tommy Norris, a crisis manager, as he navigates the intricate and perilous landscape of land acquisition, political maneuvering, and deeply personal conflicts.

The series exposes the harsh realities of the oil industry: the sacrifices demanded of its workers, the constant threat of the surrounding environment, and the ever-present dangers. Norris faces a confluence of challenges, including the volatile presence of drug cartels seeking for land control, the stringent demands of state regulations, and the heavy personal toll of his actions, all while attempting to maintain control in a world driven by greed and peril.

# **What I Liked**

- The series delivers a raw and authentic portrayal of the oil industry, showcasing the physical and emotional dangers it poses to those involved. Without resorting to overly sentimental messaging, "Landman" maintains a tight pace and suspenseful narrative, creating a palpable sense of tension and anticipation.
- Billy Bob Thornton's performance is the undeniable anchor of "Landman." He embodies Tommy Norris with a complex blend of duty, commitment, and a haunting past that fuels his search for redemption. Thornton delivers a powerful and compelling portrayal, infusing the character with intensity, conviction, and unwavering willpower.
- The stark and unforgiving West Texas setting, captured with striking cinematography, serves as a powerful backdrop to the unfolding drama. The environment's oppressive heat and relentless pressure permeate every scene, effectively drawing the viewer into this harsh and compelling world.

# **What Could Be Better**

- While the series boasts a talented cast, including John Hamm and Demi Moore, some supporting characters feel underdeveloped. Their roles, despite their importance to the overarching narrative, come across as more like brief cameos than fully realized characters, limiting their impact.
- "Landman" could have benefited from a more comprehensive exploration of the socio-economic and political implications of the oil industry. While the series touches on issues such as the climate crisis, worker safety, and the impact on local communities, these elements lack the depth and nuance needed to fully resonate. These underdeveloped storylines, while not fatally undermining the series, ultimately prevent it from reaching its full potential.
- Billy Bob Thornton's performance is undeniably strong, but his portrayal of Tommy Norris falls squarely within his established archetype. While the character is complex and well-developed, it sometimes feels like we're watching "Billy Bob Thornton in the Oil Industry" rather than a distinct and unique character.

# **My Insight**

"Landman" is a modern and compelling drama that confronts the ruthless realities of the oil industry head-on. It vividly portrays the high-stakes world of land exploitation, where fortunes are made and lost with brutal efficiency, and the profound personal sacrifices demanded by the pursuit of wealth and power.

As a Taylor Sheridan project, "Landman" inevitably draws comparisons to "Yellowstone," particularly in its exploration of power dynamics and the raw struggle for survival. However, "Landman" distinguishes itself by shifting the focus from the sweeping vistas of Montana to the harsh, industrial landscape of West Texas. While "Yellowstone" delves into the preservation of a fading way of life, "Landman" plunges into the heart of a contemporary industry driven by relentless ambition and the pursuit of black gold.

# **Rating ⭐8/10**

## A gripping drama that exposes the harsh realities of the Texas oil industry, with strong performances and a compelling narrative.
