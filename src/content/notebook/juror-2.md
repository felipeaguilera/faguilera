# **Synopsis**

Justin Kemp, a journalist and recovering alcoholic, is unexpectedly selected as a juror for a high-profile murder trial in Georgia. The case revolves around a young woman found dead at the bottom of a ravine outside a bar, where she had an intense argument with her boyfriend the night before. Initially, the case seems straightforward, but as Justin hears the testimonies, he realizes he was at the same bar that night and fears he might be involved in an accident that led to the woman's death. Caught in a moral dilemma and facing a determined prosecutor, Justin desperately seeks the truth to prevent the conviction of an innocent man and avoid a devastating outcome for himself and his family.

# **What I Liked**

1. The story presents an interesting moral dilemma, and the consequences of the events create a constant tension.
2. The narrative style is daring, presenting events from multiple perspectives, and providing intriguing details, allowing for a clever understanding of the characters' interpretations.
3. A clever critique (without resorting to caricature) of the risks and dilemmas of what we understand as justice, truth, and righteousness, providing an opportunity for an interesting discussion.

# **What Could Be Better**

1. Despite being a homicide case, the focus on the protagonist over the trial diminishes the gravity of a wrongful conviction.
2. The film failed to effectively portray the jury members; most lacked distinguishing qualities or positions that would enhance the screenplay.
3. Certain scenes and characters feel superfluous, serving only to explain the plot or clarify legal concepts.

# **My Insight**

Nicholas Hoult's convincing performance as "a good guy" makes it easy for the audience to empathize and share his disturbing and uncertain experience.

The movie goes beyond the profound moral dilemma, offering enough twists, revelations, and events that gradually clarify the happenings. This keeps the audience attentive and engaged throughout the film.

"Juror #2" is another excellent addition to Clint Eastwood's directorial career. The film bravely presents thought-provoking themes and questions, offering more than just entertainment.

# **Rating ⭐8/10**

### This captivating courtroom drama, with its thought-provoking dilemma and strong performances, proves once again that Clint Eastwood is one of the greats.

## Some Questions for Discussion

Juror #2's statement that Justice and what is Right don't always align, and that "Justice is Blind" can sometimes destroy innocent lives, raises profound questions.

The concept of the "Lesser Evil" is ever-present, prompting us to consider: **Under what circumstances might one's moral code be manipulated to avert or mitigate harm to oneself and loved ones? How reasonable is it to entrust the life of an accused person to the hands of unknown citizens?**
