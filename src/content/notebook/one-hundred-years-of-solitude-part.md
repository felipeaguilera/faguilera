# **Synopsis**

This 16-episode television series, divided into two 8-episode parts, is an ambitious adaptation. It tells the multi-generational story of the Buendia family and the fantastical world of Macondo, attempting to honor Gabriel García Márquez's acclaimed 1967 magic realism novel.

The recently released first part introduces José Arcadio Buendía and Úrsula Iguarán, cousins who marry against their family's wishes and embark on a difficult journey in search of a new home. Their adventure leads to the mythical town of Macondo, where the family and the town grow and prosper over the years. The story includes intense love, grand dreams, a frantic struggle to survive, the discovery of magical science and the complex pursuit of freedom and independence.

# **What I Liked**

1. The photography and production are top-notch, reflecting a clear commitment to a high-quality series that honors the novel.
2. The town of Macondo and its inhabitants are surprising, moving, and genuinely engaging.
3. The narrator's presence throughout the series successfully adapts beautiful written passages, inviting viewers to immerse themselves in those pages anew.

# **What Could Be Better**

1. While Macondo and its inhabitants have the potential to be surprising, this diminishes towards the end of the "First Part," becoming somewhat tedious.
2. The narrative sometimes focuses too much on only a few family members, losing the sense of a family saga.
3. There's an abrupt narrative shift from an interesting and magical family saga to an adventure of violence, revolution, and freedom, which was disappointing.

# **My Insight**

Thanks to a highly detail-oriented production, with an atmosphere that provides that humid and pure Latin American environment without falling into a caricature in an indigenous or very esoteric discourse, it manages to enchant and provide some good moments.

The arrival of One Hundred Years of Solitude as a Netflix series is undoubtedly a fantastic opportunity to get to know and recognize a well-known literary work. Personally, I hadn't been inclined to do so, and after watching the first few episodes of the series, it quickly became one of my challenges for 2025. Unfortunately, after finishing the first season, I had doubts about how it will unfold in its entirety.

# **Rating 🌟7/10**

### A carefully and well-crafted setting of a fascinating family saga recommended for fans or discoverers of the interesting town of Macondo and its inhabitants over the years.
