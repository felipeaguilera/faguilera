# Synopsis

David and Benji, who have grown apart over the years, reunite for a trip to Poland to honor their grandmother. Their journey, which begins as a simple tourist trip, evolves into a clash of personalities and an exploration of their Jewish-Polish heritage.

David, the conservative and structured one, and Benji, the impulsive and irreverent one, find themselves grappling with the weight of history and family tensions. Through a series of absurd and humorous moments, yet maintaining a profound emotional depth, the trip unveils not only their family roots but also the complexities of their emotions and the potential for reconnection.

# What I Liked

1. The close yet contrasting relationship between David and Benji creates absurd and humorous moments, which are balanced by an intimate, emotional, and deep backdrop, providing the audience with a reflective experience.
2. Kieran Culkin's performance in this film strongly resonates with his portrayal of Roman Roy in the acclaimed HBO series, Succession. Both characters exhibit a captivating blend of impulsiveness, charisma, and emotional vulnerability. Beneath their outward bravado and quick wit, there lies a palpable sense of hidden sadness and insecurity. Culkin's ability to convey these complex emotional layers with nuance and authenticity is a testament to his exceptional talent as an actor.
3. The unexpected historical journey through Poland and its World War II legacy adds a layer of tourism and serves as a backdrop for the film's photography and the dichotomy between a fun yet emotionally melancholic and sometimes painful experience.

# **What Could Be Better**

1. There is a problem with the rhythm of the film's conflicts. Undoubtedly, posing an intimate family conflict within the framework of a tourist trip, with a comedy premise, is not easy.
2. The intimate, family, and human conflict that the film attempts to portray is at times overshadowed by the exploration of Poland's rich history and cultural landscape. While the film effectively captures the personal struggles of its characters, the broader context of Poland's historical significance occasionally takes center stage. This shift in focus can detract from the emotional impact of the narrative, as the audience's attention is diverted towards the historical backdrop rather than the intimate relationships and conflicts of the characters.
3. Given the clear conflict of personalities between David, the conservative, responsible, and structured versus Benji, impulsive, irresponsible, and irreverent. It is inevitable that the second becomes the engine of events, narrative, and twists. Causing the duality that the film tries to expose to be lost, which -in my opinion- is the most interesting thing about the film.

# **My Insight**

"A Real Pain" delves into the complex emotions experienced by a Jewish couple visiting Poland. The film explores the contrast between the weight of Poland's historical significance, particularly its ties to the Holocaust, and the personal struggles of the couple.

The couple, having established themselves in the United States and belonging to different generations, each perceive and interact with the world in their own unique way. Despite their generational differences and varied life experiences, both individuals share an underlying vulnerability and a capacity for deep emotion. The film highlights the challenges they face as they navigate their personal relationship while confronting the historical and cultural complexities of their surroundings.

Being a co-production financed by the Polish government, conditions, demands, and a clear intention to promote tourism, historical weight, and Poland in general are clearly seen. However, Jesse Eisenberg manages to deliver a coherent and satisfying story.

# **Rating ⭐8/10**

### **A film that explores the complexities of family relationships on a journey through the weight of Polish history. Humor, melancholy, and memorable performances.**

## For Discussion

*It is never too late to reflect on the concept of family that we live in today. How were its origins? What were those conflicts, sometimes destructive, that led us to be who we are today? A legacy of generations, full of dreams, efforts and, why not say it, also a tender, sometimes painful ignorance.*

*"A Real Pain" invites us to look at the past with the wisdom of the present. It asks us how we are living, what we are focusing our energies on, and what we can do about it in a world where what is close feels distant. Are trips to the land of origin an attempt to reconnect with our roots, with our family, with ourselves? Or is it a forced confrontation with what we have chosen to leave behind?*
