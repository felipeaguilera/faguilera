# **Synopsis**

When 13-year-old Jamie Miller is arrested for the murder of his classmate Katie Leonard, his life and his family's are thrown into turmoil. The show explores the psychological and emotional fallout of the murder, delving into the factors that led to the tragic event. Themes of toxic masculinity, social media's influence and the difficulties of modern adolescence are explored throughout the series.

# **What I Liked**

- Owen Cooper delivers a remarkable debut as Jamie, capturing the multifaceted emotions of a teenager with overwhelming circumstances. Stephen Graham's performance of Jamie's father is also very compelling, depicting a parent's suffering and confusion with profound depth.
- The series' use of continuous one-shot episodes enhances the storytelling by creating an immersive, real-time experience. This technique intensifies the emotional impact and draws viewers deeper into the narrative, elevating the series in an amazing storytelling and technical level.
- The topics and the events surrounding "Adolescence" are very relevant and are presented in a raw and courageous way. Presenting issues such as the impact of toxic online cultures, the high pressure and expectations on young males from modern society, and the challenges faced by teenagers in today's in a difficult to confront digital age.

# **What Could Be Better**

- While the series provides an in-depth look into Jamie's life and motivations, it offers minimal insight into the victim, her family or the surroundings of the crime. While understandable, taking in consideration the scope and closeness to the characters of Jamie’s family, it feels a bit lacking with more details or views surrounding the events.
- The unrelenting emotional weight of the series, while impactful, may be overwhelming for some viewers, making it a challenging watch, specially since the limitations of the one-shot narrative, makes some dragging in the development and specially the connection between episodes a hard puzzle to engage.
- The series' ambiguous ending, while mirroring the complexities and uncertainties of real life, may leave viewers with a sense of dissatisfaction and a desire for more definitive closure and explanations. This narrative choice, while potentially thought-provoking and realistic, runs the risk of alienating viewers who crave a more conclusive and satisfying resolution to the story. The lack of clear answers and the unresolved nature of the plot threads may leave audiences feeling frustrated and unfulfilled, ultimately undermining their overall enjoyment of the series.

# **My Insight**

"Adolescence" serves as a poignant exploration of the vulnerabilities and challenges faced by today's youth. It underscores the profound influence of digital environments on adolescent development and the critical need for open communication between parents and children. The series acts as a mirror, reflecting societal issues that demand attention and dialogue.

# **Rating ⭐9/10**

A powerful and immersive crime drama that offers a very intense portrayal of modern adolescence. With exceptional performances and a fascinating and innovative cinematography.
