There was a time when every project I started began with a blank canvas and a blinking cursor in Photoshop or Figma. A logo, a wireframe, a social post—it all came from careful hands, tight grids, refined color palettes, and days of shaping pixels into meaning.

Thanks for reading Insights by Felipe Aguilera! Subscribe for free to receive new posts and support my work.

Today, the canvas still matters. The grids are still there. But before I even open a file, something else happens:  
**I ask questions. Out loud. To a machine.**

What does this brand want to say?  
What tone matches this founder's voice?  
What would a playful visual identity for a bakery look like—with a twist?

I start every creative project now with a conversation.  
A prompt. A moment of reflection where I let an AI assistant, like ChatGPT, Gemini, or Claude, challenge and stretch my thinking before I move a single layer.

It's not that I’ve stopped designing.  
It’s that I’ve added a new layer—*and it speaks back*.

---

## Prompting Isn’t Replacing Creativity – It’s Expanding It

There’s a growing fear in creative circles that AI tools will erase designers. That prompting will take the place of craft. That the spark will be outsourced.

But for me, AI hasn’t removed anything important—it’s **amplified** what matters.

Tools like ChatGPT or Lovable aren’t here to “replace” creative work. They’re mirrors. Accelerators. Co-pilots that don’t get tired or stuck. With the right mindset, I can generate ten angles instead of one. Test faster. Iterate without losing energy.

Still—none of that works without *intent*.

AI is fast, but it's not wise.  
It doesn't know what story you want to tell.  
It can't pick your voice for you.

The tools offer possibilities, but you still need to guide them. That’s not less responsibility—it’s more.

---

## A New Layer in the Creative Workflow

Prompting hasn’t replaced the canvas. It’s just made me more deliberate before I use it.

Now, when I sit down to design, I already have a rough sketch in mind. Sometimes it's a list. Sometimes it's a direction that emerged from a two-minute back-and-forth with ChatGPT. Either way, I'm not starting from zero—and I'm rarely stuck.

Prompting helps me clarify intent, uncover blind spots, and validate where I’m headed before investing in execution.

Sometimes it reveals something I hadn’t considered.  
Sometimes it just confirms I’m already on the right path.

*It’s not about shortcuts. It’s about starting with **clarity**.*

---

## I Still Design—But I’m Not Alone Anymore

This shift hasn’t made me less of a designer. It’s made me **more of a creative lead**.  
I’m not just moving pixels—I’m moving *ideas*. Faster. Sharper. With more curiosity.

And honestly, I’ve never felt more aligned with the things I care about most:  
**clear communication, simple structure, emotional clarity, and meaningful output.**

When I prompt with purpose, I’m not shortcutting the creative process. I’m enriching it.
